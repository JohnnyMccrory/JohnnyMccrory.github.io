1. Check that your AirPods are in pairing mode#

If your AirPods won’t connect to your Android phone, the first step is to check that your AirPods are in pairing mode. To do this, open the lid of the AirPods’ charging case. You’ll see a small light at the front of the case indicating that the AirPods are ready to pair. If the light is off, press and hold the setup button on the back of the case until the light begins to flash. This indicates that your AirPods are in pairing mode.

Next, navigate to your device’s Bluetooth settings page and look for the AirPods in the list of available devices. Once you find them, select the AirPods to connect. You should now be connected to your AirPods and able to use them with your Android device.

However, if you can’t find the AirPods in the list of available devices, it may be because the AirPods are not compatible with your Android phone. AirPods work with most modern Android devices, but some older or less powerful devices may not support the connection.

If you do not have access to a compatible Android device, you can still use your AirPods with other devices such as PCs, Macs, or iPhones. Just make sure that the device is compatible with AirPods before attempting to connect.
